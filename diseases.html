Retinal Diseases

Diabetic Retinopathy

Description:

Damage to the retinal blood vessels due to diabetes

Symptoms:

Blurred vision, Dark spots, Vision loss, Fluctuating vision, Floaters

Macular Degeneration

Description:

Degeneration of the macula, affecting central vision

Symptoms:

Loss of central vision, Difficulty reading

Retinitis Pigmentosa

Description:

Genetic disorder causing progressive vision loss

Symptoms:

Night blindness, Tunnel vision, Progressive vision loss, Loss of side vision, Light sensitivity, Loss of color visison

Learn More

Macular Edema

Description:

Swelling in the macula due to fluid leakage

Symptoms:

Blurred or distorted vision, Color changes

Fluid accumulation under the retina causing vision distortion

Symptoms:

Blurred central vision, Dark spot in vision, Darker or dim vision

Hypertensive Retinopathy

Retinal damage due to high blood pressure

Symptoms:

Blurred vision, Narrowed vision field

Retinal Vein Occlusion

Chalazion

Blocked meibomian gland leading to a painless eyelid lump

Symptoms:

Painless eyelid lump, Swelling, Mild irritation

Hordeolum (Stye)

Infected oil gland in the eyelid causing a painful red bump

Painful, Red bump on eyelid, Swelling, Excessive tearing

Ptosis

Drooping of the upper eyelid, often due to muscle weakness

Drooping eyelid, Vision obstruction

Eye irritation, Tearing, Redness, Eye pain, Crusting

Ectropion

Dry eyes, Irritation, Redness, Foreign body sensation, Mucus discharge

Redness, Itching, Thick discharge, Irritation, Yellow or greenish discharge, Morning crusting

Cornea Diseases

Keratitis

Description:

Inflammation of the cornea due to infection, trauma, or dryness

Symptoms:

Redness, Pain, Excessive tearing, Blurred vision, Sensitivity to light, Foreign body sensation

Learn More

Keratoconus

Description:

Progressive thinning and bulging of the cornea leading to vision distortion

Symptoms:

Blurred vision, Distorted vision, Light sensitivity, Difficulty seeing at night, Frequent prescription changes, Sudden worsening or clouding of vision

Learn More

Corneal Ulcer

Description:

Open sore on the cornea caused by infection, injury, or contact lens overuse

Symptoms:

Severe eye pain, Redness, Blurred vision, White or gray spot on cornea, Watery discharge, Sensitivity to light

Learn More

Fuchs' Dystrophy

Description:

Slowly progressing corneal disorder affecting the endothelium, leading to swelling

Symptoms:

Morning blurred vision, Glare, Halos around lights, Eye pain, Corneal swelling, Poor night vision

Learn More

Corneal Dystrophy

Description:

Group of genetic disorders causing corneal clouding and vision impairment

Symptoms:

Blurred vision, Hazy vision, Eye pain, Sensitivity to light, Foreign body sensation

Learn More

Corneal Erosion

Description:

Recurrent corneal epithelial defects causing pain and light sensitivity

Symptoms:

Sudden eye pain (morning), Excessive tearing, Blurry vision, Light sensitivity

Learn More

Blurred vision

Description:

When you can’t see things clearly, it’s likely that you’re having blurred vision. Blurred vision, or blurry vision, means that your vision isn’t sharp and crisp. Sometimes squinting will bring things into focus.

Glare sensitivity

Description:

Glare Sensitivity is a debilitating loss of visual acuity in bright lighting, such as when near a bright light source or outdoors in bright sunlight. Patients suffering from glare sensitivity will be unable to see the separate contours of brightly lit objects, and their surroundings may merge into a “wall” of bright white

Colors may appear less vibrant and more muted than before.Eye conditions that can make bright colors appear dull, faded.

Light sensitivity

Description:

Light sensitivity, also known as photophobia, is an intolerance to light that causes discomfort or pain, and can range from mild to severe

Learn More

Dim vision

Description:

Dim vision refers to a decrease in visual clarity, where objects appear less sharp and distinct, or even muted in color or with gray areas. 

Learn More

Double vision

Description:

Double vision is usually a temporary issue, but it can also be a sign of more serious health conditions. Health conditions that affect your eyes are the most common diplopia causes. That’s why it’s so important to get your eyes checked right away when you notice any changes in your vision.  

Learn More

Poor night vision

Description:

Night blindness is a symptom that makes it hard for you to see in dark places or for your eyes to adjust to sudden changes between bright light and darkness. Many conditions can cause it, and they can involve many parts of your eyes. Fortunately, most of the causes are treatable.

Learn More

Problems seeing the brightness of colors

Description:

Color Blindness. Color blindness is when you don't see colors in the traditional way because some cones (nerve cells) in your eyes are missing or don't work correctly.

Difficulty reading small text

Description:

This normal condition is called presbyopia. How does presbyopia occur? Presbyopia happens naturally in people as they age. Aging also affects muscle fibers around the lens making it harder for the eye to focus on up close objects.

Learn More

Eye strain

Description:

Eyestrain is a common condition that occurs when your eyes get tired from intense use, such as while driving long distances or staring at computer screens and other digital devices.

Learn More

Difficulty focusing

Description:

Difficulty focusing in the eyes, or blurred vision, can stem from various causes, including refractive errors like nearsightedness, farsightedness, and astigmatism, as well as eye conditions like cataracts or age-related macular degeneration.

Learn More

Problems seeing things that are close and far away

Description:

Difficulty seeing both close and distant objects can be caused by several conditions, including nearsightedness (myopia), farsightedness (hyperopia), astigmatism, and presbyopia, all of which are refractive errors
